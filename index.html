A bit about me

I'm a Computer Science grad from the University of Maryland, College Park. Most of what I do sits at the intersection of machine learning and tooling. I like building things that take a messy, menial, and manual workflow and make it feel effortless.

Over the last few years I've worked on AI content moderation at Yellow.ai, mission-critical sensor and log-analysis systems at Huntington Ingalls Industries, and medical imaging models at Qure.AI. In between, I like to work on side projects, from a phishing detection Chrome extension to a Markdown-to-PDF editor I still use weekly.

I care about the messy parts of software development: the edge cases, the memory leaks, and the API quirks nobody documents. That's usually where the interesting problems hide.
